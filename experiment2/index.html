Experiment 2 - Living Impressions

Visual Comparison

Description

In this experiment I'm trying to recreate a photo I took at Carmel Beach. I'm trying to recreate the peaceful scenery I witnessed that day procedurally. I used procedural animation to recreate the waves and the clouds moving to make the tranquil, generative beach scene feel alive and peaceful. My goal was to simulate a natural environment with dynamic elements like drifting clouds, rising sun, moving waves, and layered mountains with p5.js. This project explores how small changes in randomness and timing can create an immersive visual impression that evolves over time.

Technical

For this experiment, I used procedural animation methods to make my sunset beach scene feel alive. The sketch includes animated clouds in three different layers, a rising sun, mountains, sand, and an ocean with waves. Each time the "reimagine" button is pressed, it randomizes the scene and resets the sun's position using millis(). I used random() and a seed to generate the mountain shapes and cloud positions, and sin() to animate the wave movement. I also layered different wave colors and cloud colors to give it more depth and a soft, dreamy feel. The impression runs in real time and feels as though you're watching a sunset from a distance.

Reflection

I think my main limitation with this project was just using the common shapes in the P5 library. It's much more difficult creating a drawing with code than using traditional art and animation methods. Overall I think the experiment was super fun.
